I read this beautiful [zen koan](https://ashidakim.com/zenkoans/82nothingexists.html) this morning, really made me think about life

> "The mind, Buddha, and sentient beings, after all, do not exist. The true nature of phenomena is emptiness. There is no realization, no delusion, no sage, no mediocrity. There is no giving and nothing to be received."

freedom is realizing that you already have everything you need.

>Dokuon, who was smoking quietly, said nothing. Suddenly he whacked Yamaoka with his bamboo pipe. This made the youth quite angry.

> "If nothing exists," inquired Dokuon, "where did this anger come from?"

enlightenment is figuring out everything comes from within you.
